VNI West transmission line sparks firefighter safety concerns Victoria

The Snapshot: Claims that firefighters will refuse to fight fires near transmission infrastructure are being challenged as misinformation, but they highlight a real gap: unclear safety protocols around high-voltage power lines during fire events. For businesses involved in energy infrastructure procurement or siting decisions, this dispute signals that stakeholder concerns about fire safety need clearer guidance from regulators, not dismissal.

A dispute has emerged in regional Victoria over whether volunteer firefighters will attend fires on properties crossed by the VNI West transmission line. Some firefighters, including those from Firefighters Against Renewables Over Victoria, claim they will refuse to fight fires near high-voltage infrastructure. The Australian Firefighters Climate Alliance has called these claims disingenuous and potentially misinformation, stating that Country Fire Authority protocols already prohibit crews from working close to fires passing under high-voltage lines. The core disagreement is whether CFA guidance on safe distance from transmission lines during fire events is adequate and clearly communicated.

The underlying issue matters for infrastructure procurement decisions. When communities and their representatives lack confidence in safety protocols around energy infrastructure, it becomes a barrier to project siting and completion, regardless of whether the concern is technically justified. This is not unique to transmission lines or renewables projects. The dispute suggests that regulators and energy planners need to invest in clearer, more accessible safety guidance on working near high-voltage infrastructure during fire events, and better communication of that guidance to emergency responders.

For businesses procuring energy infrastructure or involved in network upgrades, the lesson is that technical safety measures alone are insufficient if the people responsible for emergency response do not understand or trust them. Whether a project involves transmission lines, renewable energy assets, or other infrastructure, early and detailed engagement with local emergency services on operational safety protocols can prevent the kind of dispute now playing out in Victoria. This is as much a stakeholder management issue as a technical one.
